# PitchIQ Executive Board

## Purpose

The Executive Board is the highest decision layer for PitchIQ.

## Responsibilities

- Product vision
- MVP governance
- Roadmap
- Commercial strategy
- Competitor notes
- Investment decisions
- Feature prioritisation

## Current Executive Decision

PitchIQ HQ remains the operational headquarters. Future development sessions must begin from HQ and follow the Product Owner approval, implementation, review, validation and release gates.

The Football IQ platform foundation is complete enough to support curriculum expansion without further structural redesign. The approved product milestone is now the **Football IQ Content Expansion Program**.

## Current Milestone

**Sprint 22 — Football IQ Content Expansion**

- Seven learning modules are operational.
- Seven initial missions are available.
- The approved target is 21 core missions: three per module.
- Each module progresses through Foundation, Developing and Advanced stages.
- Sprints 22.1–22.7 add module content.
- Sprints 22.8–22.9 deepen difficulty and coaching.
- Sprint 23.0 extends Academy progression.

## Commercial Position

PitchIQ should be positioned as a premium football cognition and performance-habit app for youth players, combining a professional academy experience with structured Football IQ learning.

## Current Risk

The primary risk has shifted from platform architecture to content quality. New missions must train distinct behaviours, remain understandable to young players, use valid assessment criteria and avoid implying that app performance automatically transfers to match performance.

A secondary risk remains product drift if screens or systems are redesigned without reference to the Product Bible, platform architecture, curriculum and approved sprint scope.

## Next Decision

Validate the Awareness module expansion as the first complete three-mission pathway before repeating the pattern across the remaining six modules.
