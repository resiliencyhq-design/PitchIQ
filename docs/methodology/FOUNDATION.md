# PitchIQ Foundation v1.0

**Status:** Locked

## Mission

Help every young footballer think better, learn better and play better.

## North Star

> PitchIQ develops independent football thinkers through evidence-informed assessment, purposeful practice and meaningful coaching.

## Three pillars

1. **Evidence** — design claims and measures responsibly.
2. **Development** — assessment begins the learning cycle; it is not the destination.
3. **Motivation** — every interaction should build competence, curiosity and belief in improvement.

## Four Ts

Every construct and product feature should be:

- Testable
- Trainable
- Trackable
- Transferable

## Core philosophy

- Every interaction is a coaching interaction.
- Every screen must make the player better.
- PitchIQ teaches players how to think about football, not what to think.
- Football remains representative, contextual and collaborative.
- Technology supports players and coaches; it does not replace coaching judgement.

## FIQ and MM

### Football IQ (FIQ)

- Awareness
- Game Reading
- Decision Quality
- Adaptability
- Use of Space

### Match Mentality (MM)

Inferred through behaviour in football tasks rather than a standalone questionnaire:

- Persistence
- Recovery
- Learning
- Composure
- Consistency

## Communication rules

Feedback should:

1. Celebrate something specific.
2. Explain why it matters in football.
3. Provide one actionable next step.
4. End with confidence and possibility.

Avoid fixed labels. Scores are evidence of current performance and growth, not identity.

## Governance

This document and the linked curriculum and assessment specifications form the PitchIQ Methodology v1.0 foundation lock. Any material change must be intentional, approved and versioned.
