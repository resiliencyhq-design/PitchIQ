# PitchIQ Theory of Change v1.0

**Status:** Locked

## Core belief

Young footballers can improve how they perceive, interpret, decide, adapt and reflect during representative football experiences. These capabilities are malleable through purposeful practice, high-quality feedback and repeated application.

## Development pathway

Representative football experiences  
→ representative assessment  
→ Football IQ and Match Mentality evidence  
→ personalised insight  
→ targeted training  
→ deliberate practice  
→ reflection  
→ improved football behaviour  
→ reassessment  
→ continuous growth

## Mechanisms

PitchIQ develops:

- better perception: seeing more useful information;
- better understanding: recognising football patterns;
- better decisions: choosing more effective actions;
- better adaptation: responding intelligently to change;
- better reflection: learning from success and mistakes.

## Intended outcomes

Short term: earlier scanning, improved cue recognition, stronger engagement and better recovery after mistakes.

Medium term: improved decision quality, positioning, anticipation, composure and learner independence.

Long term: more intelligent, adaptable and reflective footballers prepared for increasingly complex environments.

## Success definition

A successful player does not merely obtain a higher score. They watch football differently, think more effectively, learn more efficiently and apply improvements more consistently.

> The score is evidence of growth, not the destination.
